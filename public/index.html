Blue Slip FAQs

Common questions about AUVIS inspections in Sydney

What is a blue slip?

A blue slip is an inspection certificate required by Transport for NSW for unregistered vehicles. It verifies that your vehicle meets NSW safety and identity standards before it can be registered for the first time or re-registered after certain circumstances.

How long is a blue slip valid?

A blue slip certificate is valid for 42 days from the date of inspection. You must complete your vehicle registration at Service NSW within this period.

What's the difference between a blue slip and a pink slip?

A blue slip (AUVIS) is for unregistered vehicles before initial or re-registration. A pink slip (e-safety check) is for already registered vehicles as part of annual registration renewal.

What do I need to bring to my blue slip inspection?

You'll need photo ID (driver's licence or passport), proof of ownership (bill of sale or import documents), and your vehicle in clean, working condition.

How long does a blue slip inspection take?

Most blue slip inspections take between 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the vehicle type and condition.

Do you inspect trailers and motorcycles?

Yes, we conduct blue slip inspections for trailers and motorcycles. Call us to confirm your specific requirements.

Can you do mobile blue slip inspections?

Our inspections are conducted at our St Peters facility to ensure we have all necessary equipment and documentation. We serve all surrounding Sydney suburbs.

What if my vehicle fails the inspection?

We'll provide a detailed report of issues that need attention. Once repairs are completed, we can re-inspect your vehicle.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept cash, EFTPOS, and major credit cards for your convenience.
